⚡ Nova Tech Fest 1998 — Help Guide

Welcome to the official help documentation for Nova Tech Fest 1998. This guide contains everything you need to know about the event — from pass types to workshop schedules to frequently asked questions.

ℹ️ Quick Info NovaFest '98 runs September 12–14, 1998 at the Silicon Valley Convention Center, CA.
Theme: "Beyond the Browser." Prize Pool: $50,000 USD.

Use the Contents panel on the left to jump to any section, or scroll down to read everything. Click Register Now in the toolbar when you're ready to sign up.

⚡ What's at NovaFest '98?

NovaFest '98 is three days packed with hands-on sessions, competitive hacking, and the sharpest minds in tech. Here's a complete overview of what's on offer:

Build anything. Win $30,000. Teams of 1–4. Any stack, any idea.

🏆

💻 The Hackathon

The NovaFest Hackathon is the main event. 48 hours. Any project. Any stack. You can build a desktop app, web tool, hardware hack, command-line utility — whatever you can dream up and ship.

💡 Tip from the Judges The 1997 winning project was a distributed file-sharing tool over IRC. Think outside the box. Judges reward originality and scope above polish. Ship something weird.

Rules

  • Teams of 1–4 people (solo hackers welcome)
  • All code must be written during the event — no pre-built projects
  • Open source tools, libraries, and APIs are allowed
  • Presentations: 5 minutes + 3 minutes Q&A
  • Judged on: Innovation · Technical Depth · Presentation · Real-World Impact

❓ Frequently Asked Questions

  • You can attend solo. You can also form a team of up to 4 people. Solo hackers are eligible for all prizes. We also have a team-matching board at the venue if you want to find teammates on the day of the event.
  • NovaFest is open to all ages. Attendees under 18 must have written consent from a parent or guardian. We welcome high school students and have a dedicated Student Hacker category in the hackathon with separate prizes.
  • Bring your laptop (or desktop if you're brave), a power strip, any hardware you plan to hack with, and your government-issued ID for check-in. We provide tables, chairs, power outlets, and 56K dial-up internet access for all participants.
  • Yes. The Silicon Valley Convention Center is fully accessible. Accessible parking is available in Lot C. Please contact us in advance if you require any specific accommodations and we will do our best to arrange them.
  • Full refunds are available up to 30 days before the event (August 12, 1998). After that date, we can transfer your pass to another person at no charge. No refunds are available within 7 days of the event.
  • Yes. We have 20 dedicated 56K dial-up lines for hackathon participants, plus a T1 connection in the main lab wing (Lab Wing A). Internet access is included with all pass types at no extra cost.
  • Meals (pizza, drinks, coffee) are provided for all attendees throughout the three days. Accommodation is not provided — we recommend nearby hotels on University Ave or downtown San Jose. A room-sharing board will be available on the BBS before the event.
